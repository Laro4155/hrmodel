What's New — Live Rolling Statcast
The key upgrade in v9 is that both batter AND pitcher Statcast data are pulled fresh on every Refresh from Baseball Savant's CSV export endpoint. This means you're always looking at current form, not a season ERA that could be masking a recent hot or cold stretch.
Batter side (21-day): barrel%, hard-hit%, fly-ball%, xSLG per hitter from recent batted ball events.
Barrel % = barrels / batted ball events
Hard-hit % = exit velocity ≥ 95 mph / BBE
Fly-ball % = fly balls / BBE
xSLG = mean expected slugging across all BBE
Pitcher side (21-day): HR/9, hard-hit% allowed, fly-ball% allowed, xSLG against — computed from batted ball events allowed in the last 21 days. A starter with a 3.50 ERA but 2.1 HR/9 in his last 4 starts is a target regardless of his season number.
Why 21 days? Short enough to capture current form and mechanical changes, long enough to have ~4-5 starts (60-80 BBE) of statistical meaning. Low sample players blend in their season average.
Hitter quality gates (added v9.1): Players are capped based on their Statcast profile regardless of opponent or park. Estimated (no data): hard cap 12%. All three metrics weak (barrel <6, HH <36, FB <25): cap 13%. Low barrel + low FB: cap 16%. Extreme groundballer (FB <24): cap 17%. This prevents soft-contact hitters from ranking T1/T2 on pitcher vulnerability alone.
